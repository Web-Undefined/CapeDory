Where to Look:
Cape Dory Parts & Supplies

    On this page:

    • The most asked for CD parts and supplies
    • Related Cape Dory businesses
    • Other resources for marine parts and supplies

The CDSOA Inc. neither endorses nor recommends any of the manufacturers or suppliers listed below. They are listed here for informational purposes. Any questions or problems concerning any of the manufacturers, suppliers or their products should be directed to the individual manufacturer or supplier.


The most requested Cape Dory parts & supplies are manufactured by:

Bristol Bronze is producing the original bronze hull number builder's plates for all Cape Dory models. In addition to the builder's plates, Bristol Bronze offers replacement ports for the Typhoon and a complete inventory of bronze fittings for traditional and classic yachts. They can be contacted at PO Box 101, Tiverton, RI 02878, USA. Phone: (401) 625-5224.

Dometic AB -- the manufacturer of the original Origo 2- and 3-burner alcohol stoves/ovens found on many Cape Dory yachts. Intercon Marketing, Inc. is their U.S. distributor and they are located at 1540 Northgate Blvd., Sarasota, Florida 34234. Phone: (941) 355-4488. E-mail: info@interconmktg.com.

Edson International -- the manufacturer of the original steering systems on many Cape Dory yachts. Edson manufacturers marine steering systems and accessories, boat davits, radar towers and marine pumps...for power and sail.

Ocean Engineering, the original supplier of stainless steel tubular fabrications, is no longer in business. For new rails and other stainless steel fabrications, try White Water Marine, 3755 West Water Street, PO Box 610595, Port Huron, MI 48060; Tel: 810-987-4837. Brooks Marine Fabrication, 1219 11th St, Building B, PO Box 1262, Anacortes, WA 98221; Tel: 360-299-2956; Email: info@brooksmarinefab.com. Stainless Outfitters, Inc., 161 Saunders Road, Barrie, Ontario L4N 9A3; Tel: 705-725-1779 or 1-800-268-0395; Email: sales@stainlessoutfitters.com.

Pompanette, Inc. -- the manufacturer of the original Bomar hatches found on many Cape Dories. They are also the manufacturers of the Pompanette line of fishing and marine hardware, e.g., fighting chairs, helm seats, rocket launchers, etc.; Hood Yacht Systems furling systems; Bomar/Gray hatches, portlights, deckplates, etc.

Rig-Rite -- Spartan Spars, the original equipment masts for Cape Dory Yachts, are manufactured and distributed by Rig-Rite.

Spartan Marine -- the manufacturer of the original bronze fittings found on Cape Dory Yachts, located at: HC 33, BOX 1460, Georgetown, ME. 04530. Phone: (207)371-2542 Or 1-(800)325-3287 and ask for a catalog.

St. Angelo Hardwoods -- though they no longer have the patterns or mouldings specific to Cape Dory boats, they are still a source of marine teak lumber, decking and mouldings and other hardwoods. They are located at 490 Eagleville Road, Tiverton, RI 02878. They can be reached at 401-624.3900 or 888-253-9663.

Taunton Stove Company, Inc. -- the manufacturer of the original Tasco 2- and 3-burner propane stoves/ovens found on many Cape Dory yachts. Tasco Engineering/Tasco Marine Divisions, 490 Somerset Avenue, North Dighton, MA 02764. Phone: (508)-823-0786. E-mail: tasco@ici.net

Triad Trailers, which was a supplier of trailers to Cape Dory and its dealers, is still in business in New Milford, Connecticut. They have custom trailer specs for the Typhoons and most Cape Dory sailboats to 30’. Triad can be reached at (860) 354-1146 or triadtrls@aol.com.

Westerbeke Corporation (Hansen Marine Engineering; find a dealer in your area), Yannar Diesel America Corp. (Mack Boring & Parts Company, find a dealer in your area), Volvo Penta (find a dealer), Perkins Engine Co., Ltd. (find a dealer), and Perkins-Sabre (find a dealer) -- the manufacturers/distributors of many of the original marine diesel engines found in Cape Dorys.

Other manufacturers/suppliers of original Cape Dory equipment. Is that part no longer available? Try one of these alternative manufacturers.


Related Cape Dory Businesses:

C.W. Hood Yachts, Inc. -- Hood has the mold for the 24-foot trawler. They can be contacted at: PO Box 443, 65A Front Street, Marblehead, MA 01945
. Phone: (781) 631-0192. E-mail: info@cwhoodyachts.com

Compass Yachts -- Compass Classic Yachts has introduced the new Hurricane, a classic redesign of the Alberg-designed Cape Dory Typhoon. They can be contacted at: P. O. Box 143, South Orleans, MA 02662. Phone: (508) 240-1032. E-mail: info@compassclassicyachts.com

Hobby Marine -- Builders of the Cape 300 Motor Sailer -- originally the Cape Dory 300 Motor Sailer. They can be contacted at: P.O. Box 220, Pollocksville, NC 28573. Phone: (252) 224-1600.

Nauset Marine -- Nauset Marine is building custom motoryachts from the original CD 30, 33 and 42 molds. They also are believed to have the molds for the 19, 22 30 and 33 foot sailboats. They can be contacted at: Route 6A, P.O. Box 357, Orleans, MA 02653. Phone: (508) 255-0777. E-mail: info@nausetmarine.com

Robinhood Marine Center -- Builders of the Robinhood 36, 40 and 45 sailboats as well as the Robinhood 33 Motoryacht all from the original Cape Dory molds (Cape Dory essentially became Robinhood after reorganization in 1990). They are located at: HC 33 - Box 1460, Georgetown, ME 04548, USA. Phone: (207) 371-2343 or (800) 255-5206, Fax (207) 371-2899. E-mail: info@robinhoodmarinecenter.com

Stroudwater Boatworks -- Builder of the Handy Cat 14. Phone: (207) 730-2108. Email: brian@stroudwaterboatworks.com

Triangle Marine Engineering, Ltd. -- Builders of the Hadley 25, 26 and Hadley 27. These boats are being constructed using the original molds from the Cape Dory 25D, 26 and the Dieter Empacher designed Cape Dory 270. They are located at 87 Airport Street, Bldg. #20, Quonset Davisville Commerce Park, North Kingstown, RI 02852. Phone: (401) 295-7773. E-mail: TRIMARENG@aol.com
